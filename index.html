INTRODUCTION

Background, Mar 28, 2023

Life expectancy is a critical indicator for assessing the overall health of a population. This crucial metric captures the mortality rate across an individual's lifetime, reflecting the average age of death within a specific group. Emphasizing a detailed visualization analysis of life expectancy is vital, as it sheds light on the health conditions of a population and guides strategic planning and resource allocation in healthcare services. Additionally, it can influence government decisions regarding program funding and investments.

Despite considerable improvements in global life expectancy, significant disparities still exist between countries, with a 30-year gap separating the highest and lowest life expectancies. Identifying and understanding the key factors influencing life expectancy is crucial for shaping national policies, particularly in countries with lower life expectancies.

The primary goal of our visualization application is to effectively explore and identify potential risk factors or areas where health interventions could be focused to improve life expectancy outcomes, offering valuable insights to inform policy decisions aimed at addressing these disparities. The project will focus on visually representing and analyzing a WHO dataset with health intervention-related features, helping users better understand the underlying factors and their relationships.
